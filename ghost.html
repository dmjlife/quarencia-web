The Context
The world has
already changed.
TFR EU exchanges must now collect and report the identity of both sender and receiver on every transaction. In force 2024-25.
MiCA All crypto service providers must be licensed. Compliance costs are forcing exchanges out of the EU, reducing available on-ramps.
AMLR Proposed outright ban on anonymous exchange accounts. Restrictions on privacy coins. Reporting thresholds on self-hosted wallets.
6AMLD Criminal liability extended to crypto. 22 predicate offenses. False positives now carry real legal exposure.
The Strategy
Not a ban.
A cage.

The strategy is not to ban self-custody. It is to make every convenient entry and exit point a permanent identity checkpoint.

Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, and Revolut are now legally required to log and share your wallet addresses, balances, amounts, and transaction timestamps with regulators. Most users have no idea this is already happening. They treat these platforms like banks. They are not banks. They are surveillance nodes.
The regulated financial system is being rebuilt around permanent identification. The only question is whether you are inside that system — or outside it.
The Response
Three principles.
No compromises.
I
Maximize Privacy
No customer database. No purchase trail. No address linkage. No metadata leakage to third-party nodes. You cannot report what you do not hold. Ghost is anonymous by architecture, not by policy.
II
Maximize Security
The private key is generated offline, exists only in temporary memory, and is erased on power-off. There is nothing persistent to steal. All signing occurs in total physical isolation. The attack surface is structurally minimal.
III
Eliminate Third-Party Risk
No company in the custody chain. No entity that can be subpoenaed, breached, regulated, or coerced. No third party means no third-party risk — by definition, not by trust.
The Problem with "Good Enough"
Ledger secures the device.
It cannot secure the company.
Customer Database
Ledger maintains a full customer database — names, addresses, emails. The 2020 breach exposed 272,000 users. That data still circulates.
Seed Extraction
The "Recover" feature proved definitively that Ledger firmware can extract your private key and transmit it to external servers. This was not theoretical.
Purchase Trail
Every Ledger purchase leaves a permanent record: name, address, payment method — permanently linked to a known hardware wallet. Identity-to-wallet linkage is baked in.
Closed Firmware
The firmware is closed source. You cannot audit it. You must trust Ledger's assurances — and those assurances are subject to regulatory compulsion.
Ledger is a fine product for a world that no longer exists. It cannot escape the corporate and regulatory layer it belongs to.
Why Ghost Operates at a Different Level
Five advancements
most wallets never attempt.
01 Own Bitcoin Node
Your addresses, balances, and IP stay invisible.
Most users — including Ledger users — connect to third-party nodes run by Coinbase, Electrum, or Blockchain.com. Every balance check leaks identity to corporate infrastructure. Ghost runs its own node. Nothing leaves.
02 Stateless Signer
The key exists in memory. Power off — it vanishes.
Hardware wallets store keys in a persistent secure element — extractable by firmware, as Ledger's "Recover" proved. Ghost's signer holds nothing between sessions. There is no key to steal, copy, or compel.
03 True Air-Gap via QR
No wire. No radio. No attack surface.
USB connections create firmware attack vectors. Bluetooth creates interception risk. Ghost communicates exclusively via QR codes — camera in, screen out. The only data channel is light.
04 Multisig as Default
No single device or location controls the funds.
Single-signature wallets mean a single point of failure. Ghost runs 2-of-3 multisig across geographically dispersed signers — at a cost lower than one Ledger. Compromise one device: nothing moves.
05 Zero Purchase Trail
No identity linked to any wallet component.
Ledger ships to your door — name, address, and payment method permanently linked to a known wallet. Ghost is built from generic Raspberry Pi parts: bought anonymously, assembled privately, owned invisibly.
